As the wealthiest state in the U.S., Connecticut is short of affordable housing options.
What's affordable housing?
For a housing unit to be considered affordable, it should cost no more than 30% of the monthly income of a family’s earning slightly less than the Average Median Income.
Income and affordable housing avaialability
Within the state, the higher the median household income, the lower the percentage of affordable housing units.
The rate hasn’t improved. In fact, 99 towns have a smaller share of housing deemed affordable from 2002 to 2018, the most recent year of available data.
