Income and affordable housing avaialability
An affordable housing unit is one that costs no more than 30% of the monthly income of a family earning slightly less than the Average Median Income (80% of AMI). This is achieved largely by public financing, in the form of government subsidies and credits, as well as price or deed control in Connecticut. Fewer than a fifth of Connecticut towns have more than 10% of housing units that are affordable.
